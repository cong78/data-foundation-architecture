# Onboard a Data Domain

<div class="decision-brief"><div><small>Use when</small><strong>A business boundary needs product accountability.</strong></div><div><small>Decision</small><strong>Register, enable with conditions, or reject.</strong></div><div><small>Owner</small><strong>Domain owner with foundation governance.</strong></div><div><small>Output</small><strong>Domain record, controls, services, and improvement plan.</strong></div></div>

## Preconditions

| Required | Evidence |
| --- | --- |
| Business purpose and sponsor | Named outcome, funding intent, and accountable sponsor. |
| Candidate boundary | Concepts, capabilities, exclusions, sources, consumers, and overlaps. |
| First use case | A real source-to-consumer outcome suitable for proving adoption. |

## Actions

| Step | Action and owner | Evidence |
| ---: | --- | --- |
| 1 | **Domain owner:** define purpose, boundary, portfolio intent, and non-goals. | Draft domain record. |
| 2 | **Architecture and governance:** test overlap, stability, accountability, and policy context. | Boundary and admission decision. |
| 3 | **Domain owner:** assign portfolio owner, steward, technical lead, risk contact, support, and escalation. | Accepted role assignments. |
| 4 | **Foundation platform:** select service profiles, identities, environments, quotas, support tier, telemetry, and cost allocation. | Enablement plan. |
| 5 | **Assessor and domain owner:** baseline maturity and fund the highest-risk gaps. | Maturity record and improvement plan. |
| 6 | **Domain team:** deliver the first product through shared ingestion, creation, access, observability, and operations services. | Product go-live and adoption evidence. |

## Stop Conditions

- No accountable domain owner or portfolio decision authority.
- Boundary duplicates an existing domain without a resolved ownership decision.
- Domain requires a parallel ingestion, identity, policy, or support path without an approved exception.
- Regulatory, residency, security, or operating obligations cannot be met.

## Done Criteria

- Stable domain id, boundary, owners, governance context, and first portfolio are registered.
- Required foundation services, identities, support, telemetry, cost, and escalation are enabled.
- Admission gates pass and maturity gaps have owners and dates.
- At least one real product demonstrates the domain operating model.
